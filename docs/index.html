Overview

What is CAR-T Therapy?

Chimeric Antigen Receptor T-cell (CAR-T) therapy is a form of adoptive cell immunotherapy where a patient's own T lymphocytes are genetically re-engineered to express a synthetic receptor — the CAR — that confers MHC-independent recognition of tumour-associated antigens. Once re-infused, these living drugs proliferate, hunt, and kill cancer cells with remarkable precision and long-lasting memory.

How the Treatment Works — Step by Step

Leukapheresis

T-cells are collected from the patient's blood via apheresis — isolating billions of white blood cells.

🔬

Activation

Isolated T-cells are activated ex vivo with anti-CD3/CD28 beads or artificial APCs to initiate proliferation.

🧫

Gene Engineering

A lentiviral or retroviral vector delivers the CAR gene into the T-cell genome, stably expressing the receptor.

⚗️
04

Expansion

CAR-T cells are expanded in bioreactors to hundreds of millions of cells over 10–14 days.

💉
05

Lymphodepletion

Patient receives fludarabine + cyclophosphamide conditioning to clear space and reduce immune rejection.

🎯
06

Infusion & Kill

CAR-T cells are infused IV. They engraft, expand in vivo, recognise tumour antigen, and destroy cancer cells.

Molecular Biology

Anatomy of a CAR Molecule

Each CAR is a modular protein spanning the T-cell membrane, coupling extracellular antigen recognition to intracellular T-cell activation machinery.

🎯

scFv — Antigen Recognition

Single-chain variable fragment derived from a monoclonal antibody. Fuses V_H and V_L chains via a flexible peptide linker. Recognises tumour antigen in an MHC-independent fashion — the key advantage over native TCRs.

🔗

Hinge + Transmembrane

Spacer region (CD8α or IgG-derived) provides flexibility for antigen binding. Transmembrane domain (CD28, CD8α, or CD3ζ) anchors the CAR and influences dimerisation, CAR stability, and immune-synapse formation.

⚡

Co-stimulatory Domains

4-1BB (CD137): Promotes long-term persistence, memory formation, and oxidative metabolism.
CD28: Drives rapid proliferation and effector function but may reduce persistence.

🔥

CD3ζ — Activation Signal

Contains 3 immunoreceptor tyrosine-based activation motifs (ITAMs). Upon phosphorylation, recruits ZAP70 → LAT → downstream T-cell activation cascade → cytotoxic granule release (perforin/granzyme B) and cytokine secretion.

Engineering Evolution

Five Generations of CAR Design

Each generation adds functional sophistication to improve persistence, potency, safety, and ability to overcome the immunosuppressive tumour microenvironment.

1st Gen
~1989

CD3ζ Only

Original design by Eshhar et al. Contained only the CD3ζ signalling chain. Produced weak, short-lived anti-tumour responses. Limited IL-2 production; poor persistence in vivo. Largely abandoned clinically.

scFv Hinge TM domain CD3ζ
2nd Gen
~2002 · Dominant

+ Single Co-stimulatory Domain (CD28 or 4-1BB)

Added one co-stimulatory domain (CD28 or 4-1BB). Dramatically enhanced proliferation, persistence, and cytokine production. All 7 currently FDA-approved CAR-T therapies are 2nd-generation. 4-1BB confers superior long-term memory; CD28 drives faster initial expansion.

scFv Hinge TM 4-1BB or CD28 CD3ζ
3rd Gen
~2010

+ Dual Co-stimulatory Domains

Combines two co-stimulatory domains (e.g., CD28 + 4-1BB or OX40). Increased cytokine secretion and proliferation, but paradoxically can trigger tonic signalling and premature exhaustion. Currently dominant in market share (41% of next-gen trials as of 2024).

scFv Hinge TM CD28 4-1BB / OX40 CD3ζ
4th Gen
TRUCKs ~2014

Armored CARs / TRUCKs — Cytokine Payload

T cells Redirected for Universal Cytokine Killing. Engineered to constitutively or inductively secrete pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-12, IL-15, IL-18, IL-21) at the tumour site. This recruits and activates innate immune cells (DCs, NK cells, macrophages) to amplify anti-tumour immunity — critical for solid tumours and overcoming immunosuppressive TME. Fastest growing segment; expected highest CAGR through 2030.

scFv Co-stim ×1–2 CD3ζ IL-12/15/18 payload NFAT promoter
5th Gen
Emerging ~2020+

Synthetic Cytokine Receptor Integration

Incorporates a truncated cytokine receptor domain (e.g., IL-2Rβ fragment) that activates JAK/STAT signalling in addition to the co-stimulatory and CD3ζ cascades. Provides three synergistic activation signals for superior in vivo persistence and self-sufficiency. Also includes advanced gene-editing strategies: CRISPR-Cas9 knockout of PD-1, TET2, REGNASE-1 to prevent exhaustion.

Safety Profile

Key Toxicities & Management

🌡️

Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS)

Most common toxicity. Mediated by massive cytokine release (IL-6, IFN-γ, TNF-α) from activated CAR-T cells and bystander immune cells. Presents with fever, hypotension, hypoxia. Graded 1–4. Managed with IL-6 receptor blockade (tocilizumab) and corticosteroids.

Incidence severity:
Grade ≥3 CRS: ~10–25% across approved products
🧠

ICANS (Neurotoxicity)

Immune effector cell-associated neurotoxicity syndrome. Ranges from confusion/aphasia to cerebral oedema. Mediated by BBB disruption, macrophage activation, and endothelial inflammation. Managed with corticosteroids; anti-seizure prophylaxis for severe cases.

Incidence severity:
Grade ≥3 ICANS: ~5–30% (higher with CD28 co-stim)
🩺

On-Target Off-Tumour Toxicity

CAR-T cells attack normal cells expressing the same antigen (e.g., B-cell aplasia from anti-CD19). Manageable with IVIG, but highlights need for tumour-specific targeting. New strategies: logic-gated CARs, antigen density thresholds.

Clinical impact:
🔄

Antigen Escape & Exhaustion

Tumour cells downregulate target antigen to escape CAR-T killing. CAR-T cells also develop an exhausted phenotype (high PD-1, LAG-3, TIM-3). Solutions: dual/triple targeting CARs, PD-1 knockout (CRISPR), armored cytokine payloads, shorter manufacturing preserving stemness.

2026 & Beyond

Next Frontiers in CAR-T

The field is undergoing a renaissance, expanding beyond autologous blood cancers into allogeneic products, solid tumours, and autoimmune diseases.

🏭

Allogeneic "Off-the-Shelf"

Donor-derived CAR-T eliminates the 3–6 week autologous manufacturing wait. CRISPR knockout of TCR, MHC-I, and checkpoint genes prevents GvHD and host rejection. Allogene projects 30,000–60,000+ doses/year at <$10–20K COGS. Program-defining 2026 readouts anticipated.

💊

In Vivo CAR-T Generation

Lipid nanoparticle (LNP) or viral delivery of CAR mRNA/DNA directly into the patient's body — eliminating ex vivo manufacturing entirely. Companies including Umoja, Capstan, and In Vivo Therapeutics are in IND-enabling studies.

🧩

Solid Tumour CAR-T

The next major frontier. Challenges: antigen heterogeneity, immunosuppressive TME, poor trafficking/infiltration, T-cell exhaustion. Strategies: armored IL-12/18 payloads, logic-gated CARs, PD-1 KO, locoregional delivery, TME-remodelling co-therapies.

🤖

AI-Driven CAR Design

Generative AI platforms accelerating target nomination, scFv optimisation, receptor expression prediction, and combinatorial domain selection. Reduces design cycles from years to months. Multiple companies (Exscientia, Absci, Generate:Biomedicines) active in 2025–2026.

🛡️

Autoimmune Diseases

Landmark reports in 2024–2026: deep remissions of treatment-refractory SLE, SSc, and RA with CD19-targeting CAR-T. CD19-directed B-cell depletion resets the autoreactive immune repertoire. Ongoing academic and industry trials. A paradigm shift beyond oncology.

✂️

CRISPR-Enhanced CAR-T

Gene knockout of PD-1, LAG-3, TET2, REGNASE-1 to prevent exhaustion. Insertion of CAR into safe-harbour loci (TRAC, B2M) for uniform expression. Multiplexed editing (5+ gene edits) now demonstrated safely in Phase 1 trials.

🌡️

Safer Toxicity Profiles

Reducing ITAM number on CD3ζ (1 vs 3), using GM-CSF knockout, switchable/suicide gene CAR-T (iCasp9), and low-dose prophylactic tocilizumab all show promise for reducing CRS/ICANS without compromising efficacy.

💰

Access & Cost Reduction

Current autologous prices: $373K–$465K per infusion. Allogeneic, point-of-care manufacturing (CliniMACS Prodigy, Ori Biotech), and shortened manufacturing (5–7 days vs 14) expected to reduce costs dramatically. Market projected at $7.24B in 2026, reaching $13.78B by 2031 (CAGR 13.7%).
